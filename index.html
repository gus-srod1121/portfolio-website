I'm a Computer Systems Technology student at BCIT with a strong focus on game and interactive software development. I enjoy building gameplay systems, experimenting with mechanics, and iterating quickly through prototyping and playtesting.

Most of my work comes from game jams, personal projects, and school assignments, where I've developed games both solo and in small teams using Unity and C#. I'm especially interested in systems-driven design, responsive controls, and creating tight gameplay loops supported by original visuals and music.

I have experience developing games through multiple game jams and personal projects, primarily using Unity and C#. These projects range from short 3-day jam games to longer-term works in progress.

Through game jams, I've worked under tight deadlines, rapidly prototyped mechanics, and iterated based on playtesting. I've developed solo and team projects, contributing to gameplay systems, controls, visual effects, audio and music.

My experience also includes version control, collaboration, and scoping features realistically for small teams and short timelines.
